Dirt-Cheap Ramen

Description

Here's a little something to keep you warm and fed during spring! Using native Californian plants, we're taking classic dorm food into a whole new realm with no added cost!
Unless you use eggs, in which case you're financially screwed.

Chickweed and cranesbill are hardy volunteer plants that can be found in both urban and rural areas. They are texturally interesting and mild in taste, which balances the tangy broth and tender noodles. An egg lends umami, completing the dish.

Safety note: This recipe requires foraging, which is a learned skill. Do not attempt without a proper guide and positive identification!

Ingredients

  • (1 block) Ramen noodles
  • (½ packet) Ramen salt packet
  • (1 handfull) Chickweed and/or cranesbill
  • (4 cups) Filtered water
  • (1 tsp) Rice wine vinegar (sub: ¼ tsp apple cider)
  • (½ tbsp) Sesame oil (sub: olive oil)
  • (1 tsp) Ginger powder
  • (1 pinch) Ground pepper
  • (2) Clean eggs

Steps

  1. Harvest tender greens using a sharp knife. Rinse well and set aside.
  2. Add water, ½ salt packet, and vinegar to a soup pot. Bring to a light boil.
  3. Using a ladle, drop in the un-cracked eggs. Boil for 3 minutes.
  4. Add the greens. Boil for 1 minute.
  5. Add the ramen noodles and oil. Boil for 1 minute.
  6. After the eggs have been in for 6 minutes, they're done. Remove from the broth and cool on a clean towel.
  7. Take the pot off the stove.
  8. Run the eggs under cool water. Roll on the towel to carefully crack and peel the shell.
  9. Ladle soup into the bowl. Once you're ready to eat, add an egg.
  10. Enjoy!
