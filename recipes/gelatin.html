Dense Tea Gelatin

Description

Well here's something different! A chewy, savory gelatin dish that may be entirely my own making. This easy and novel treat will take your mind off our crapsack world, like you're a depressed zoo animal in dire need of enrichment. And really, aren't we all?

I won't lie, this one's mostly about texture, otherwise you'd just drink a cup of tea. But why drink water when you could bite it?

Ingredients

  • (4 bags) Earl grey tea
  • (4 tbsp) Unflavored gelatin
  • (3 cups) Filtered water
  • (1 cup) Cold coconut milk
  • (⅛–¼ cup) Sugar, to taste

Notes

You can use any type of herbal tea, but savory is best. Matcha is excellent, but you may need to add more sugar to balance the bitterness.

Any kind of milk works just fine for this recipe.

Steps

  1. Pour the cold milk into a mixing bowl.
  2. Sprinkle the gelatin over the milk. Go slowly!
  3. Boil the water, add sugar, and steep your tea.
  4. While the tea steeps, mash the bloomed gelatin with a whisk to break up any clumps.
  5. Wait until the tea is just cool enough to sip, remove the bags, then pour into the mixing bowl.
  6. Whisk the mixture until the gelatin is dissolved.
  7. Cool on the counter-top, then refrigerate for 2–4 hours.
  8. Enjoy chewing your tea!
